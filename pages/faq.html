General AI GEO FAQs

What is AI GEO? ▼

AI GEO (AI Generative Engine Optimization) is the practice of optimizing your digital presence to appear in AI-generated responses, recommendations, and search results. As AI tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity become primary research tools, AI GEO ensures your brand is mentioned, cited, and recommended when these platforms answer questions about your industry, products, or services.

Unlike traditional SEO that focuses on ranking in search engine results pages, AI GEO optimizes for being cited and recommended within AI-generated content itself.

How is AI GEO different from traditional SEO? ▼

While traditional SEO optimizes for ranking in search engine results, AI GEO focuses on:

  • Direct Citations: Being mentioned within AI-generated answers rather than just ranking in link lists
  • Content Quality: AI models prioritize authoritative, comprehensive content over keyword optimization
  • Structured Data: Schema markup helps AI understand context and relationships
  • Natural Language: Conversational, question-based content performs better
  • E-E-A-T Signals: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness are critical

Many SEO best practices still apply, but AI GEO requires additional optimization specifically for how AI models consume and cite information.

Which AI platforms should I optimize for? ▼

Priority depends on your audience and industry, but we recommend starting with:

  1. ChatGPT: Most widely used conversational AI with SearchGPT integration
  2. Google Gemini: Deep integration with Google Search and AI Overviews
  3. Microsoft Copilot: Bing integration and enterprise reach
  4. Perplexity AI: Search-focused with strong citation emphasis
  5. Voice Search: Alexa, Siri, and Google Assistant for local and voice queries

See our platform-specific guides for detailed optimization strategies for each.

How long does it take to see results from AI GEO? ▼

AI GEO results can vary based on several factors:

  • Quick wins (1-2 weeks): Schema markup, FAQ optimization, and content structure improvements
  • Medium-term (1-3 months): Building authority through quality content, backlinks, and social signals
  • Long-term (3-6+ months): Establishing domain authority and consistent citations across platforms

AI models update their training data periodically, so continuous optimization and fresh content are important for sustained visibility.

Do I need technical skills to implement AI GEO? ▼

No, but it depends on the depth of optimization:

  • No technical skills needed: Content optimization, social media presence, review management
  • Basic HTML knowledge: Adding schema markup, improving semantic HTML
  • Web development skills: Advanced technical SEO, site architecture, performance optimization

We provide templates and tools to help non-technical users implement many optimizations. See our Templates & Checklists for ready-to-use resources.

Can AI GEO hurt my traditional SEO performance? ▼

No, AI GEO and traditional SEO are complementary. AI GEO best practices include:

  • High-quality, comprehensive content (good for SEO)
  • Structured data and schema markup (helps SEO)
  • Fast page speeds and mobile optimization (SEO ranking factors)
  • Building authority and backlinks (critical for SEO)
  • Natural, user-focused language (what Google recommends)

In fact, many AI GEO practices improve traditional SEO performance because both prioritize user value and content quality.

Technical Implementation FAQs

What is schema markup and do I really need it? ▼

Schema markup is structured data that helps AI models (and search engines) understand the context, relationships, and meaning of your content. It's highly recommended because:

  • Improves AI comprehension: Makes it easier for AI to extract and cite accurate information
  • Enhanced search features: Enables rich snippets, knowledge panels, and featured snippets
  • Better categorization: Helps AI classify your content correctly
  • Competitive advantage: Many competitors don't implement schema properly

Priority schema types for AI GEO:

  • Product - For e-commerce and product pages
  • Article - For blog posts and content
  • FAQPage - For FAQ sections
  • Organization - For business information
  • LocalBusiness - For local businesses

See our Schema Markup Guide for implementation examples.

How do I add schema markup to my website? ▼

There are several ways to implement schema markup:

  1. JSON-LD (Recommended): Add a script tag with JSON data to your page head or body
  2. CMS plugins: Use WordPress plugins like Yoast, RankMath, or Schema Pro
  3. Google Tag Manager: Deploy schema through GTM for easier management
  4. Manual HTML attributes: Add microdata directly to HTML elements

Example JSON-LD for an FAQ:

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "What is AI GEO?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "AI GEO is..."
    }
  }]
}
</script>

What's the difference between robots.txt and meta robots tags? ▼

robots.txt (site-level):

  • Controls crawler access to entire directories or files
  • Located at your domain root (example.com/robots.txt)
  • Prevents crawlers from accessing specific URLs
  • Does NOT prevent indexing if other sites link to blocked pages

Meta robots tags (page-level):

  • Controls indexing and following links on specific pages
  • Added to HTML <head> section
  • Options: noindex, nofollow, noarchive, etc.
  • More precise control over individual page behavior

For AI GEO, you generally want AI crawlers to access and index your content. Use noindex only for pages like thank-you pages, duplicate content, or internal search results.

How important is page speed for AI visibility? ▼

Page speed is very important for AI GEO because:

  • Crawl efficiency: Faster pages allow AI crawlers to access more content
  • User experience: AI platforms prefer citing sites with good UX
  • Mobile performance: Critical for voice search and mobile queries
  • SEO ranking factor: Affects traditional search rankings, which influence AI citations

Target metrics (Core Web Vitals):

  • LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): < 2.5 seconds
  • INP (Interaction to Next Paint): < 200ms
  • CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): < 0.1

Use tools like PageSpeed Insights to measure and improve performance.

Can AI crawlers access JavaScript-rendered content? ▼

It depends on the crawler, but many AI crawlers have limited JavaScript execution:

  • Google's crawlers: Can execute JavaScript but with limitations
  • Other AI crawlers: Many read only the initial HTML without JavaScript execution
  • Performance impact: JS-heavy sites may not be fully crawled due to resource limits

Best practices:

  • Use server-side rendering (SSR) or static site generation (SSG) when possible
  • Keep critical content in the initial HTML
  • Progressive enhancement: content works without JavaScript
  • Avoid hiding navigation or important content in Shadow DOM
  • Test with "View Page Source" to see what crawlers see

Troubleshooting

Why isn't my content showing up in AI responses? ▼

Common reasons and solutions:

  1. Insufficient authority:
    • Build backlinks from reputable sources
    • Increase E-E-A-T signals
    • Create more comprehensive, authoritative content
  2. Content not indexed:
    • Check robots.txt isn't blocking crawlers
    • Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
    • Ensure no noindex meta tags
  3. Poor content quality:
    • Expand thin content with more depth
    • Add unique insights and original research
    • Improve readability and structure
  4. Technical issues:
    • Fix crawl errors in Search Console
    • Improve page speed
    • Ensure mobile-friendly design
  5. Training data lag:
    • Some AI models use older training data
    • Focus on platforms with real-time search (ChatGPT SearchGPT, Perplexity, Gemini)
My schema markup isn't validating. What should I do? ▼

Follow these steps to fix schema validation errors:

  1. Use official validators:
    • Schema.org Validator
    • Google Rich Results Test
  2. Common errors and fixes:
    • Missing required fields: Add all required properties for your schema type
    • Invalid JSON syntax: Check for missing commas, brackets, or quotes
    • Wrong data types: Ensure URLs are URLs, dates are in ISO format, etc.
    • Nested objects: Make sure nested properties follow proper structure
  3. Use schema generators:
    • Start with our schema templates
    • Use online schema generators for complex types
    • Validate before deploying to production
AI is citing incorrect information about my brand. How do I fix this? ▼

Correcting misinformation in AI responses requires a multi-pronged approach:

  1. Update your official sources:
    • Ensure your website has current, accurate information
    • Update Wikipedia if your brand has a page
    • Correct information on social media profiles
    • Update Google Business Profile and other directories
  2. Create authoritative content:
    • Publish official statements or press releases
    • Create comprehensive "About" pages with correct information
    • Use structured data to define key facts
  3. Build authority signals:
    • Get coverage from reputable news sources with correct information
    • Build backlinks to your authoritative content
    • Encourage reviews and testimonials that reflect accurate information
  4. Report to platforms:
    • Use platform-specific feedback mechanisms
    • ChatGPT: Use the feedback thumbs down button
    • Google: Submit feedback through Search Console
    • Contact platform support for persistent issues
  5. Monitor and maintain:
    • Regularly test brand queries across AI platforms
    • Keep information fresh and up-to-date
    • Document the correct information prominently

My site is slow. What are the quickest wins? ▼

Quick performance improvements for AI crawlability and user experience:

  1. Image optimization (immediate impact):
    • Compress images using tools like TinyPNG or ImageOptim
    • Use modern formats (WebP, AVIF) with fallbacks
    • Implement lazy loading for below-the-fold images
    • Set proper width and height attributes to prevent layout shift
  2. Enable caching (easy win):
    • Browser caching for static assets
    • CDN for global content delivery
    • Server-side caching if using a CMS
  3. Minimize CSS and JavaScript:
    • Remove unused CSS and JavaScript
    • Minify and combine files
    • Defer non-critical JavaScript
  4. Upgrade hosting if needed:
    • Shared hosting can be slow; consider VPS or dedicated hosting
    • Enable HTTP/2 or HTTP/3
    • Use a performance-optimized server stack

Test with PageSpeed Insights and implement top recommendations first.
