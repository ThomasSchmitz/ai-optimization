DeepSeek Model Architecture Overview

DeepSeek's models combine Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) routing, transformer efficiency techniques, and hybrid data governance to deliver competitive performance with lower infrastructure costs.

  • Sparsely activates specialized experts per token for cost savings.
  • Supports domain experts (legal, finance, creative) selected in real time.
  • Exposes control tokens to emphasize reasoning vs. creative outputs.

Optimization Strategies for DeepSeek

Combine content, data, and engineering strategies to unlock accurate, compliant DeepSeek outputs.

1. Curate Domain Knowledge Hubs

  • Cluster documents by vertical (finance, retail, legal) with bilingual summaries.
  • Standardize metadata: region, regulatory regime, customer segment.
  • Link supporting media (charts, product sheets) with alt text in both languages.

2. Structure Retrieval Pipelines

  • Use hybrid dense + sparse indexing to capture Chinese characters and Pinyin keywords.
  • Implement domain-specific embedding models for improved recall.
  • Apply document-level guardrails before injecting into context windows.

3. Design Bilingual Prompts

  • Frame instructions with parallel Chinese/English directives when clarity matters.
  • Specify tone, formality, and target region (e.g., Mainland vs. Hong Kong).
  • Include fallback instructions for regulatory deflection or escalation to humans.

4. Fine-Tune with Human Feedback

  • Recruit bilingual reviewers to rate accuracy, sensitivity, and cultural fit.
  • Capture structured rubrics: clarity, compliance, empathy, domain correctness.
  • Iterate with reinforcement learning or supervised fine-tuning on failure cases.

5. Instrument Quality Monitoring

  • Log prompts, completions, and retrieval sources for auditability.
  • Detect hallucinations via citation verification and contradiction checks.
  • Trigger automated alerts for sensitive topic mentions or refusal spikes.

6. Integrate Multimodal Assets

  • Leverage DeepSeek's vision capabilities for product recognition workflows.
  • Provide transcription-ready audio datasets with industry-specific vocabulary.
  • Pair text prompts with structured data tables for deterministic responses.

Prompt & Retrieval Patterns that Work

Combine structured prompts with retrieval templates to produce dependable outputs.

1. Dual-Language Framing

Provide instructions in both English and Chinese to reduce ambiguity.

  • Introduce the task with Chinese context, then mirror in English for clarity.
  • Explicitly specify measurement units, currency, and desired output structure.
  • Use delimiter tokens to separate instruction, context, and examples.

2. Retrieval Blueprints

Design repeatable retrieval templates for consistent responses.

  • Prefix prompts with [REGULATED], [MARKETING], or [TECH] tags for routing.
  • Append citations including URL, publish date, and ICP/record numbers.
  • Cache embeddings for FAQ content to minimize latency spikes.
