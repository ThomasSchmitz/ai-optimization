Product Names & Titles

Product names and titles are the first thing AI systems analyze. Descriptive, category-inclusive names significantly improve AI understanding and discoverability.

2. Add Key Differentiators

  • "Noise-Cancelling Bluetooth Headphones"
  • "4K Webcam with Auto-Focus"

3. Use Searchable Keywords

  • "Sony WH-1000XM5 Wireless Noise-Cancelling Headphones - Black"

Product Descriptions

Product descriptions should be informative, scannable, and packed with the information AI needs to understand and recommend your product.

Structure for Product Descriptions

1. Opening Summary (50-100 words)

Start with a concise paragraph covering:

  • What the product is
  • Who it's for
  • Primary use case
  • Key benefit or differentiator

Example:

"The ErgoChair 3 is a premium ergonomic office chair designed for professionals who spend 6+ hours daily at their desk. Featuring adjustable lumbar support, breathable mesh back, and 4D armrests, it reduces back pain and improves posture. Supports up to 300 lbs with a 10-year warranty."

2. Key Features List

Use bullet points for easy scanning:

  • Adjustable Lumbar Support: Customizable lower back support prevents pain
  • Breathable Mesh: Mesh back keeps you cool during long sessions
  • 4D Armrests: Adjust height, angle, depth, and pivot
  • Synchronized Tilt Mechanism: Recline smoothly from 90¬∞ to 135¬∞

3. Detailed Explanation (150-300 words)

Expand on features with context and benefits:

  • How features work
  • Why they matter
  • Real-world use cases
  • Comparison to alternatives

Features vs. Benefits

AI understands both features (what the product has/does) and benefits (why it matters). Include both for complete product representation.

Features vs. Benefits

Feature (What it is) Benefit (Why it matters)
256GB SSD storage Fast boot times and app loading
IP68 water resistance Safe to use in rain or near water
Adjustable lumbar support Reduces lower back pain
20-hour battery life Work all week without recharging

Writing Both Effectively

Template: [Feature] provides/enables/delivers [Benefit]

  • "The 4K resolution provides crystal-clear image quality for video editing"
  • "Dual-zone climate control enables driver and passenger to set different temperatures"
  • "Automatic firmware updates deliver new features without manual installation"

Use Cases & Target Customers

Help AI understand who your product is for and what problems it solves. This enables more accurate recommendations for specific user queries.

Target Customer Segments

Explicitly state who the product is designed for:

  • "Ideal for remote workers and home office professionals"
  • "Perfect for small businesses with 5-50 employees"
  • "Designed for gamers who need low-latency wireless connectivity"
  • "Great for students and budget-conscious users"

Common Use Cases

Describe specific scenarios and applications:

  • "Use for video conferencing, content creation, or gaming"
  • "Perfect for organizing projects, tracking tasks, and collaborating with teams"
  • "Works great for email marketing, customer segmentation, and automation workflows"

üí° Match AI Queries

Users ask AI: "Best laptop for college students", "Desk chair for tall people", or "Budget microphone for podcasting". Include these scenarios in your product content.

Pricing & Availability

Clear, accurate pricing information is critical for AI shopping assistants. Include all relevant pricing details to help AI answer user queries accurately.

What to Include

  • Current Price: "$299" or "Starting at $99/month"
  • Original Price (if on sale): "Regular price: $399"
  • Currency: Specify currency (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.)
  • Availability: "In stock", "Ships in 2-3 business days", or "Pre-order"
  • Shipping: "Free shipping" or "Shipping from $5.99"
  • Return Policy: "30-day money-back guarantee"
